About Eiffel Tower

HISTORY

02

The Eiffel Tower was designed by French engineer Gustave Eiffel and constructed for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World’s Fair) held to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution.

03

The design was initially met with criticism from prominent artists and intellectuals who believed the iron structure was an eyesore, but it eventually became a symbol of French ingenuity.

04

The Eiffel Tower was the tallest man-made structure in the world until the completion of the Chrysler Building in New York in 1930.

01

Intro of Eiffel Tower

Location:

Located in Paris, France, on the Champ de Mars near the Seine River.

Construction:

Built between 1887 and 1889 for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World’s Fair) to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution.

Architect:

Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel and his company, although much of the design work was done by Maurice Koechlin and Émile Nouguier.
